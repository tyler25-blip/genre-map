Atlas
Installation · 2026 Document № 01
An interactive music installation

A room of knobs,
a table that
plays like vinyl.

Atlas is a single room. Twenty rotary knobs on the wall, each one a genre. Visitors take the knobs they love, turn each to weight their preference, and set them on a round table. Every visitor's rotations fold into one shared playlist — and the whole room spins the table together, like a turntable, to play it.

Form
Single-room installation
Duration
5–12 minutes per visitor · open-ended collective
Inputs
20 detachable knobs · 1 rotating round table
Outputs
Projected atlas · one shared playlist · 2.1 audio
Built with
TouchDesigner · Arduino · p5
§ 01 · Sequence

One continuous gesture,
in four beats.

Beat 01

Pick

Lift a knob off the wall. Each is a genre, none are labelled.

Beat 02

Place

Set the knob on the round table, inside the projected atlas.

Beat 03

Weight

Twist the knob. How far you turn it is how much you love the genre.

Beat 04

Spin

The room spins the table together. Forward plays the mix, backward rewinds it.

§ 02 · The Wall

Twenty knobs, twenty genres.

A wall of twenty rotary knobs, mounted at eye level in a loose grid. Each knob is a genre. None of them are labelled.

Visitors lift the knobs they're drawn to — as many or as few as they like. A magnetic socket releases each one with a gentle detent; the wall leaves a quiet gap where a knob used to be.

Count
20 detachable rotary knobs
Mount
Magnetic socket · contactless rotary read
Label
None — identity lives in position, not text
§ 03 · The Weight

How far you turn it is how much you love it.

The knob in your hand is continuous — not a vote, not a preset. Rotate it all the way and the genre leans hard into the mix; barely touch it and it sits quietly at the edge.

Every visitor's rotations are summed in real time. One playlist emerges from the room: weighted, collective, never the same twice. Your turn is a small push inside a shared tide.

Range
270° of rotation, continuous, no snap
Weighting
Summed across all visitors, normalised live
Output
One shared playlist, generated and regenerated
§ 04 · The Table

A round table, an atlas, the knobs you chose.

A circular oak table, roughly a metre across. A projector above paints the atlas of genres onto its surface — curves, nodes, gentle gradients.

Visitors set their picked knobs directly onto the region of the atlas that represents them. Jazz lives here; hip-hop lives there. The table becomes the group's collective playlist, physically arranged by hand.

Surface
Ø 1000 mm oak, low-friction bearing beneath
Tracking
Capacitive read per socket · IR overhead
Projection
Atlas rendered live on the tabletop
§ 05 · The Spin

The table is the turntable.

Once the knobs are in place, the whole table rotates — the room spins it together, like hands on a DJ's platter.

Forward spin plays the shared mix. Faster spin, faster playback. Reverse spin rewinds it, vinyl-style. Stop, and the music holds its breath with you.

Drive
Free-spinning bearing, optical encoder beneath
Response
Angular velocity → playback rate, linear
Act
Takes several hands to keep it going — deliberately so
AT REST Playback 0.00× ↻ drag anywhere on the disc to spin — forward to play, backward to rewind
§ 06 · Principles

Five
design rules.

01

Mechanic first

The interaction teaches the concept. Text only labels what is already visible.

02

Nothing is labelled

Knobs carry no names. Genres sit in positions, not words. Meaning lives in relation.

03

Weight by hand

Rotation is commitment. The further you turn, the more of yourself you leave in the mix.

04

One playlist, many hands

Nothing here belongs to one person. Every rotation folds into a single collective mix.

05

Listening is a performance

The spin is a group act — the music only plays while the room keeps the table turning.
